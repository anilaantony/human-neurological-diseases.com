SPINA BIFIDA

Spina bifida is a birth defect that occurs when the spine and spinal cord don't form properly. It's a type of neural tube defect. The neural tube is the structure in a developing embryo that eventually becomes the baby's brain, spinal cord and the tissues that enclose them.Typically, the neural tube forms early in pregnancy and it closes by the 28th day after conception.

SIGNS AND SYMPTOMS

  • Spina bifida occulta
  • Meningocele
  • Myelomeningocele

CAUSES

Doctors aren't certain what causes spina bifida. It's thought to result from a combination of genetic, nutritional and environmental risk factors, such as a family history of neural tube defects and folate (vitamin B-9) deficiency.

RISK FACTORS

  • folate deficiency
  • family history of neural tube defects
  • some medications
  • diabetes
  • obesity
  • increased body temperature

COMPLICATIONS

  • walking and mobility problems
  • bowel and bladder problems
  • accumulation of fluid in the brain
  • shunt malfunction
  • infection in the tissues surrounding the brain
  • Tethered spinal cord
  • sleep disordered breathing
  • skin problems
  • latex allergy
  • other complications

PREVENTION

  • get folic acid first
  • planning pregnancy
